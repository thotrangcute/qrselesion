Frequently Asked Questions

>

What is a QR code?

A QR code (Quick Response code) is a type of barcode that can be scanned using a smartphone or QR reader to quickly access information such as websites, contact details, and more.

>

How does QR code work?

A QR code works by encoding data into a two-dimensional pattern of squares that can be read by a camera or scanner, then decoded into readable information like URLs or text.

>

How do I create a QR code?

Step 1: Select your QR code type: You may choose from URL, vCard, Plain Text, Facebook, WiFi, and Google Review.

Step 2: Fill all the details: Enter all the information we require that appears. Then, select “Generate.”

Step 3: Download your QR Code.

>

Are QR codes free to use?

All of DingDoong’s QR Codes are free. Our small note: you won't be able to modify the information, so always test and make sure they work before printing.

>

Can QR codes be customized?

Yes, QR codes can be customized with different colors, logos, and designs to match your branding while still remaining scannable.

>

How long do QR codes last?

QR codes do not expire, and they can last indefinitely as long as the printed code remains intact and scannable.

>

What are the benefits of using QR codes?

QR codes offer quick access to information, enhance user engagement, are easy to create and use, and can store a variety of data types.

>

Do QR codes require an internet connection to work?

QR codes themselves do not require an internet connection to be scanned, but accessing the encoded information (like a website) may require an internet connection.

>

How do I ensure my QR code is scannable?

To ensure your QR code is scannable, use high contrast colors, keep the design simple, avoid resizing too small, and test the code with multiple devices before distribution.
